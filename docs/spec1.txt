PRODUCT &amp; TECHNICAL SPECIFICATION
Personal Budget App — for Gary &amp; partner
Native cross-platform app (React Native + Expo), local-only storage, built for a couple&apos;s monthly credit-card budgeting ritual.

1. Purpose &amp; Core Workflow
This app digitizes the exact process already in use on paper, without changing the ritual — just removing the arithmetic and adding visibility.
The monthly ritual, as-is
Sit together and log every transaction from the month, one by one, from each credit card statement/memory.
Each transaction is tagged to a card and a spending category.
Total spend per category is checked against what was planned for that category (the &quot;ideal sheet&quot;).
Total monthly spend is subtracted from salary.
Fixed amounts for savings goals are subtracted next.
What&apos;s left is the surplus.
What the app changes
All of the above happens automatically as transactions are entered — no manual subtraction.
Historical trends become visible (this app makes that possible; a paper sheet doesn&apos;t).
Both of you can review the same numbers on one phone, in real time, while talking through the sheet together.
2. Design Direction
Apple-native visual language — the app should feel like it belongs on iOS next to Wallet, Health, and Numbers. Not a generic cross-platform look.
Visual principles
Minimal surface area: generous white space, no visual clutter, one primary action per screen.
SF Pro (system font) throughout; Dynamic Type support (respects the user&apos;s iOS text-size setting).
Native iOS color system: system background colors, semantic colors (systemRed for over-budget, systemGreen for surplus/under-budget), full support for Light and Dark Mode via system appearance, not a custom toggle.
Cards and lists use iOS-standard rounded corners (continuous corner radius, ~14pt), subtle shadows, standard 16pt margins.
Tab bar navigation (bottom), matching Wallet / Apple&apos;s own finance-adjacent apps — not a hamburger menu.
Numbers are the hero: large, bold, tabular (monospaced-digit) figures for amounts so columns of numbers align — this is what Apple&apos;s own Numbers and Stocks apps do.
Category colors: a fixed, tasteful palette (12–14 hues) assigned once per category, used consistently across charts, icons, and lists so a category is recognizable by color alone.
Charts rendered natively (Swift Charts if we go fully native later; for the Expo/RN build, Victory Native or React Native Skia — both render smooth, native-feeling charts, not web-embedded ones).

3. Information Architecture — Screens
3.1 Home / Dashboard (default tab)
The single-glance monthly summary. This is what you open the app to see.
Header: current month + year, with left/right arrows to move between months.
Surplus hero card: large number showing this month&apos;s surplus (green) or shortfall (red), with the formula visible beneath it in small text: Salary − Spend − Savings Goals = Surplus.
Quick stats row: Total Spent, Total Saved, Days Left in Month.
Budget health strip: one thin horizontal bar per category showing actual vs. ideal limit, sorted worst-to-best (most over-budget first) so problems are visible immediately.
Recent transactions: last 5 entries, tap to see all.
Floating &quot;+&quot; button: always-visible quick-add for a new transaction.
3.2 Transactions Tab
Chronological list, grouped by day, newest first.
Each row: category icon (colored), merchant/description, card used (small badge), amount.
Filter bar at top: filter by card, by category, by date range.
Search bar for merchant/description text.
Swipe-to-delete, tap to edit.
Add Transaction sheet (modal): Amount (numeric keypad), Category (grid of icons), Card (horizontal picker of your saved cards), Date (defaults to today, editable — since you often log a batch after the fact), optional Note.
Bulk-entry mode: after opening &quot;+&quot;, a &quot;save &amp; add another&quot; button keeps the sheet open with the same date/card pre-filled, so logging a whole statement in one sitting is fast — this maps directly to how you currently do it with your partner.
3.3 Budget Tab (the &quot;ideal sheet&quot;, digitized)
List of all categories, each row editable: category name, icon, color, and monthly ideal limit amount.
Add/remove/reorder categories.
Each category row also shows this month&apos;s actual spend inline, with a progress bar (green under limit, amber near limit, red over limit).
Tap a category to drill into its own detail screen: this month&apos;s transactions in that category, a small trend chart of that category over the last 6 months, and the limit editor.
Salary &amp; Savings Goals section (same tab or a sub-section): Monthly salary input (can be a fixed number or edited each month if it varies). Savings goals list — name + fixed monthly amount (e.g., &quot;Emergency fund — $500&quot;, &quot;Vacation — $200&quot;). Add/remove goals freely.
3.4 Cards Tab
Visual list of your 4–5 credit cards, styled like Apple Wallet cards (name, last 4 digits, a chosen color/gradient — no real card numbers or sensitive data stored, purely a label).
Tap a card to see all transactions on that card, this month or filtered by range, plus a running total for that card.
Add/edit/remove/reorder cards.
3.5 Insights / Charts Tab
Monthly trend line: total spend over the last 6–12 months.
Category breakdown donut/pie chart for the selected month, tap a slice to see that category&apos;s transactions.
Budget vs. Actual bar chart: one bar pair per category, ideal limit vs. actual, for the selected month.
Card usage breakdown: how much was spent on each card this month (bar or donut).
Surplus history: bar chart of surplus/shortfall per month over time, so trends in your saving rate are visible.
Month picker/range picker at top of this tab, applies to all charts on the page.
3.6 Settings
Currency selection.
Export data (CSV) — for backup, since data is local-only.
Import data (CSV) — to restore from a backup or migrate from your existing sheet.
Manage categories/cards shortcuts (same as Budget/Cards tabs).
App appearance: follows system Light/Dark automatically (no manual override needed, but can add one if requested later).
Face ID / passcode lock toggle (optional privacy layer, since it&apos;s financial data).
4. Data Model
All data lives only on your device (local SQLite database via Expo&apos;s SQLite module). No account, no login, no server, no analytics — matches how you described wanting to use it.

5. Core Calculations
These formulas run live, recalculated the moment any transaction, salary, or savings goal changes — nothing is ever manually re-added.
5.1 Monthly Surplus (the Home tab hero number)
Surplus = Salary
          − SUM(Transactions.amount WHERE month = selected month)
          − SUM(SavingsGoals.monthlyAmount)
5.2 Category Actual Spend
CategorySpend[c] = SUM(Transactions.amount
                       WHERE categoryId = c AND month = selected month)
5.3 Budget Health (per category)
Remaining[c] = Category.monthlyLimit − CategorySpend[c]

Status:  green  if CategorySpend[c] &lt;= 80% of monthlyLimit
         amber  if 80–100% of monthlyLimit
         red    if over 100% of monthlyLimit
5.4 Card Total
CardTotal[card] = SUM(Transactions.amount
                      WHERE cardId = card AND month = selected month)
5.5 Trend series (Insights tab)
Each of the last 6–12 months is computed with the same formulas above, producing a time series for the trend line, the surplus-history bars, and category trend charts.

8. Explicitly Out of Scope for v1
Bank/card account linking or automatic transaction import (Plaid, etc.) — you&apos;re entering manually by design, matching your current ritual.
Multi-device sync or shared accounts — confirmed single-device, local-only.
Income tracking beyond a single monthly salary figure — no multiple income streams in v1.
Debt/loan tracking, investment tracking — pure budgeting scope only.
Month-to-month variable category limits — confirmed fixed limits are sufficient for now; can be added later without a data-model rewrite (MonthlySettings could absorb per-category limits if ever needed).
9. Open Questions for the Build Session
Small decisions worth confirming in Claude Code before or during the build, so nothing gets assumed:
Exact category list and starting ideal limits (utility, grocery, home, personal/clothing, others — full list + numbers).
Exact card names/colors for the 4–5 cards.
Currency (assumed USD unless specified).
Whether monthly salary is truly fixed or varies — affects whether MonthlySettings needs a UI or can just be a single global settings value.
Names and fixed amounts for current savings goals.
